Aegis Mechanized detachment

The Aegis detachment is the mechanized division of the 332nd. They consist of 3 groups of 3 members that run in armored vehicles. They help to support the ground forces by taking out large armored presences and large droid presence, acting as a mobile medical assistance base, and a hard-hitting group that can push ahead in situations where troopers can not push forward. They also assist Reaper by taking out anti-air guns.



Positions and Responsibilities

There are a lot of positions in the Aegis detachment that you can be slotted in. The positions are listed below:

Crewman

The Crewman position is an interchangeable position within Aegis. They can be a driver,gunner,JTAC, or medic based on the mission and who all are playing. These roles are designed to allow members to flip between them and try out each area. There are 2 crewmen per vehicle, 6 total in a section.

  1. Follow the orders of the Crew Lead (or the Section Lead in given circumstances) and communicate information.
  2. Gunner: The gunner's role is to operate the brunt of the vehicle's weaponry and should only engage cleared targets through the commander's discretion unless in the most extreme scenarios.
  3. Driver: The driver's role is to move the vehicle in accordance with the commander's guidelines, this position is also tasked with staying aware of immediate vehicle surroundings while helping with target acquisition.
  4. JTAC: The driver or gunner will be tasked with monitoring LR:32 and keeping the vehicle commander informed.
  5. One of the crewmen will also be the crew medic and will be responsible for maintaining the health and safety of the crew.

Crew Lead (Vehicle Commander)

Crew leads are responsible for a single-vehicle of crewmen (either Alpha, Bravo, or Charlie). The vehicle commander will be the deciding force behind where the crew moves and what it engages, they are responsible for helping the crew with threat acquisition while also monitoring the attached squad SR, and the Aegis SR/LR. They will communicate and pass information between their vehicle and the squad lead. They will also issue orders to the crewmen running with them. There is one crew lead per vehicle, 2 total on a squad.

  1. Issue orders to your crew based on the orders of the section lead
  2. Step up to section leader should the section lead go unconscious/die, including stepping up on the long-range radio to communicate with other ground forces

Section Lead

The section leader's (SL) job is to manage the entire section. These individuals tend to micromanage LESS and focus more on general orders to complete current tasks which allows the Crew Leaders(CLs) to be creative in completing said tasks.

  1. Lead the section based on incoming ground information and decisions made in the planning stage with the platoon staff
  2. Communicate updates over the long-range ground channel (70) to ensure your squad and others are up to date on what is happening
  3. Monitor the additional Platoon LR channel for proper support cohesion.
  4. Relay orders to the crew leads, who will pass this information on to their crews to complete
  5. Keep an up to date roster of your section inside your section folder
  6. Update the promotion board each week with any member that is due for a promotion
  7. Ensuring all administrative documents (including, but not limited to this document) are kept up to date
