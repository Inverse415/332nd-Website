How to Join

Reaper Aviation Detachment

The Reaper Forces are the aviation portion of our legion. They take care of vultures and other air-related enemies. They also provide the ground forces with support via transportation, resupply, and CAS strikes.



Positions and Responsibilities

There are a lot of positions in the Reaper forces that you can be slotted in. The positions are listed below:

Aviator

These are the general pilots. Their primary goal is to follow the orders of the team lead to accomplish each mission objective and cover the ground forces. There are 6 pilots per flight commander, including the section lead.

  1. Follow the orders of the Team Lead (or the Squad Lead in given circumstances) and communicate information via your team radio (Not squad radio).

Section Leader

The role of a Section Lead is to make sure their section is trained and prepared to take on any aviation task. Section leaders also need to be prepared to run as FAC in times when a flight commander is not present.

  1. Step up to FAC should FAC go down, including stepping up on the long-range air radio (32) to communicate with ground forces.

Flight Commander

The role of a Flight Commander is to ensure that their sections are getting trained and to lead the flight during main operations.

  1. Lead Reaper based on incoming information and decisions made in the planning stage with the platoon staff
  2. Communicate updates over the long-range air channel (32) to ensure your squad and others are up to date on what is happening
  3. Relay orders to pilots to complete
  4. Keep an up to date roster of your squad inside your squad folder
  5. Update the promotion board each week with any member that is due for a promotion
  6. Ensuring all administrative documents (including, but not limited to this document) are kept up to date
