Gravedigger Engineer Detachment

Gravedigger is our Engineering Detachment of the 332nd. They help keep our piolets in the air with ammo and fuel so our ground forces have support. They are also available to send long-range missiles when air can not reach a target due to anti-air. They are a key support group for the legion that assists everyone.



Positions and Responsibilities

There are a lot of positions in the Gravedigger Detachment that you can be slotted in. The positions are listed below:

Landing Pad Engineers (LPE)

LPEs help to refuel, rearm, and repair incoming air assets. They also help with general tasks that may come up during the mission. They are like a jack-of-all-trades and specialize in other areas as well.

  1. Refuel, Rearm, and repair incoming air assets
  2. Stay on 333 SR and assist with other tasks as needed
  3. Help defend the Venator from incoming forces that may attempt to overtake it

Air Traffic Control (ATC)

Air traffic control. They keep track of who, what, and where the air units are and where they are landing. They help get troops off the main deck and onto the mission field during step-off and re-groupings as well.

  1. Direct air units to their pads during landing
  2. Usually the gravedigger leader or someone Zeus trained
    • They are responsible for spawning in special air units
    • Adding vehicle skins
  3. Sets up units before step off prior to mission start
  4. Stay on LR 33 and SR 333 and 332 during the mission

UAV Operator (AWAC)

AWACs handle observation and early warning for both air and ground units. They are the eyes in the sky that help keep everyone safe.

  1. Must be trained in UAV flight
  2. Keep an eye on ground and air forces, providing warnings via 70 LR and 32 LR if they have incoming forces
  3. Stay on 333 SR during the mission

Artillery Operator

They work out of firebases controlling and communicating with ground force JTACs to send artillery strikes out as needed. They can often reach areas air can not and play a key role in assaults.

  1. Requires JTAC training and Artillery training
  2. Stay on 32.6 LR and listen for incoming JTAC Ground transmissions requesting transmission
  3. Work with JTACs to send HE, Cluster Mines, Cluster Shells, and Cluster AV to targets
  4. Send out Mortars to defend the fire-base
  5. Stay on 333 SR during the mission
