Torrent Ground Forces

The Torrent ground forces are the unit's “boots on the ground”. They are deployed to the necessary objectives and can accomplish a wide range of tasks, from capturing an HVT (High-Value Target) to clearing out a city. The Torrent forces have many slotted positions, each with their own tasks and responsibilities to ensure the squad runs smoothly and that each mission is a success.



Positions and Responsibilities

There are a lot of positions in the Torrent forces that you can be slotted in. The positions are listed below:

Trooper

These are the general infantry. Their primary goal is to follow the orders of the team lead to accomplish each mission objective. There are 2 teams on each squad, with 3 troopers per team (6 total).

  1. Follow the orders of the Team Lead (or the Squad Lead in given circumstances) and communicate information via your team radio (Not squad radio)

Medic

Medics are the medical staff of the ground force. Their primary goal is to keep the squad alive and in good health. There is one medic per team (2 total).

  1. All of the above tasks
  2. Complete the Medic Training and monitor the medical channel for any updates to our mods that may affect your equipment
  3. Ensure the health and safety of your team, prioritizing the second medic, squad lead, Jtac, team leads, then troopers
  4. Assist the second medic with their team, should the need arise
  5. Stay on the medical radio net (77) and report any MAS CASs with the radio and purple smoke

JTAC

JTACs serve as the communicators between the squad and the air forces (referred to as Reaper forces). JTACs usually run with the squad leader, unless the squad lead sets them to run with a team. There is only one JTAC per squad.

  1. Complete the JTAC training and monitor the JTAC Channel for changes that could affect your job
  2. Stay on the long-range air channel (32) and respond to all calls to your squad
  3. Communicate any needs of your squad to Reaper FAC, including but not limited to CAS strikes, Resupplies, Redeployments, Pick-ups

Team Lead

Team leads are responsible for a team of troopers and a medic (either Alpha or Bravo). They will communicate and pass information between their team and the squad lead. They will also issue orders to the troopers and medic running with them. There is one team lead per team, 2 total on a squad. Squad Lead

The role of a Squad Lead (SL) is to manage an entire squad. These individuals tend to micromanage LESS and focus more on general orders to complete current tasks which allow the Team Leaders (TL's) to be creative in completing said tasks.

  1. Lead the squad based on incoming ground information and decisions made in the planning stage with the platoon staff
  2. Communicate updates over the long-range ground channel (70) to ensure your squad and others are up to date on what is happening
  3. Relay orders to the team leads, who will pass this information on to their teams to complete
  4. Keep an up to date roster of your squad inside your squad folder
  5. Update the promotion board each week with any member that is due for a promotion
  6. Ensuring all administrative documents (including, but not limited to this document) are kept up to date

Weapon Restrictions

There are restrictions on certain weapons/accessories each squad can have. These are listed below:

  1. 2x Dedicated AT Troopers (Maximum of 1 PLX)
  2. 2x Support Troopers (Maximum of 1 15L)
  3. 2x Grenadier Troopers
  4. 2x Marksman Troopers
  5. 2x Medics
  6. 3x Squad shields (designated by the squad lead)
  7. 3x disposable rockets
