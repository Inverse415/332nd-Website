C-Shop

These are specific areas of the unit that are necessary for the unit to survive. C-Shops are where individuals who have talents to contribute to the unit or want to learn talents to benefit the unit can utilize them. C-shops are open to anyone wanting to be involved, although some do require more trust (i.e discord/TeamSpeak admin) we are always willing to train individuals in new techniques or allow them to use already existing ones to benefit our community. To become involved in C-Shops reach out to the leads of each C-Shop which can be found on the roster.


C-Shop Leadership

C-Shop Leadership

C-shop leaders (CWS) are the head of one of 5 sections of our C-shops. Each CWS is in charge of at least one section. They oversee the section head(s) below them and work with the C-Shop Commander and Sub-Commander to ensure the sections are running smoothly. Learn More: C-shop Leadership

C-Shop Commander

The C-Shop Commander role is being able to run any/everything within C-Shops. This individual should be able to run every C-Shop and answer any questions in relation. This role is a non-leadership role in-game, rather than a leader within C-Shops. Learn More: C-Shop Commander

C-Shop Sub Commander

The C-Shop Sub Commander role is to help the C-Shop Commander run any/everything within C-Shops. This individual should be able to run every C-Shop and answer any questions in relation and step up if the C-Shop Commander is absent. This role is a non-leadership role in-game, rather than a leader within C-Shops. Learn More: C-Shop Sub-Commander

C-1 Personnel

Consists of roster clerks who work “behind the scene” to help keep the unit organized and running smoothly. There is a lot to keep track of in our unit, especially with new members coming in every week, promotions, and our many project groups. Roster clerks help keep everything documented. Learn More: C-shop Leadership

C-2 Operations

C-2 consists of campaign staff, story writers, mission builders and zeus. Campaign staff act as a director of sorts. Story writers design missions and campaigns for our unit. Mission builders create the maps we use for our missions. Zeus act like a “game master” in real-time. Learn More: C-shop Leadership

C-3 Training

C-3 consists of the unit's trainers. This includes initial trainers, medical trainers, JTAC trainers, mortar trainers, training development team, and any other training positions that are added for specific purposes. Initial trainers work to help train incoming recruits. Medical trainers work to help train members on how to properly perform medical duties in op. JTAC trainers work to help train members on how to run as a JTAC. Mortar trainers teach members how to use the mortar. The Training Development Team creates new training materials. Learn More: C-shop Leadership

C-4 Communications

C-4 consists of the unit's media team, Machinima staff, and the news staff team. The media team is in charge of all the media accounts the legion owns. The news team is in charge of writing the bi-weekly newsletters. Machinima Project Team wants to create a series using the 332nd Arma unit, creating our own; story, lore, and anything needed for our project. Learn More: C-shop Leadership

C-5 Resources

C-5 consists of the aux mod staff, as well as the server management staff. The Aux team is in charge of creating mods for our unit. All our custom armor, weapons, and vehicle textures are their work. They also ensure all our resources are balanced to create an incredible Arma experience. The server staff is in charge of our Servers. They turn on/off, update, restart, and keep our servers running smoothly. Learn More: C-shop Leadership

C2-Operations

Campaign Staff

Campaign staff act as a director of sorts. They are responsible for getting the story started, passing that to the story writers for getting fleshed out, and giving the mission builder(s) and Zeus(s) a direction for playing out the missions in a way that follows the story without giving the details away to them. Learn More: Campaign Staff

Story Writer

Story writers design missions and campaigns for our unit. They are responsible for designing the lore and objectives to hand off to mission builders for creation. You should not make something so detailed that it limits the mission builders and Zeus(s), but they should have direction. Learn More: Story Writer

Zeus

Zeus(s) runs all of our missions. They act like a “game master” in real-time. They are the ones sending droids and air forces during missions. They work to create a fun, balanced mission that leads to a constant flow of challenges for the members on the ground. Learn More: Zeus

Mission Builder

Mission builders create the maps we use for our missions. They build amazing buildings and change towns to fit the story being told. Without them, we would lose so much immersion. Learn More: Mission Builder

Event Management

Event management helps to set up both Arma-related and unrelated events. They set up event servers in Arma, help to run charity events, and run other big events for our unit. Learn More: Event Management

C3-Training

Training Development Team

The training development team is in charge of creating new training documents and keeping our current documents up-to-date. Mods change (and so do mechanics and key bindings). As these changes occur, updates to current documents may be necessary. We are also growing and we will need new training documents created and these need to be reviewed with the trainers. Learn More: Training Development Team

Initial Trainers

Initial trainers work to help train incoming recruits. They help ensure that buttons are mapped correctly, show off the weapons and enemies we have, explain the basics of our unit, and teach much, much more.

Medical Instructors

Medical trainers work to help train members on how to properly perform medical duties in op. This helps prepare them in both their trooper roles and if they step up to a medic role. They help ensure that buttons are mapped correctly, explain each type of medical equipment, explain the priority of who to heal, and teach many other tips and tricks.

JTAC Instructors

JTAC trainers work to help train members on how to run as a JTAC. JTACs are the communicators between the ground forces and the air forces. They have to be able to broadcast airstrikes, redeployments, resupplies, and other important pieces of information. JTAC trainers ensure that these calls are sent in the correct format and ensure that JTACs can properly communicate so there is not a fatal breakdown in a mission.
