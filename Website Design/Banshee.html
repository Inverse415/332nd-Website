Banshee Recon Forces

The Banshee Squads are the Recon forces of the unit. They help provide the platoon with battlefield intelligence, ranging from objectives to droid presence. They are deployed before the platoon and their recon is vital for the ground and air forces. The main purpose is being the forward attachment that gathers vital information and support for the General Infantry. Whenever they're ahead of General Infantry they're going to be their eyes and ears.



Positions and Responsibilities

There are a few positions in the Banshee forces that you can be slotted in. The positions are listed below:

Trooper

These are often considered “general infantry”, though Banshee is anything but general. Trooper's primary job is to be a gunner of sorts and help back up the team should they be engaged. There is 1 trooper in each squad.

  1. Follow the orders of the Squad leader and communicate information via your team radio

Medic

Medics are the medical staff for the squad. Their primary goal is to keep the squad alive and in good health. There is 1 medic per squad.

  1. All of the above tasks
  2. Complete the Medic Training and monitor the medical channel for any updates to our mods that may affect your equipment
  3. Ensure the health and safety of your team, prioritizing the squad lead, Jtac, then troopers.
  4. Stay on the medical radio net (77) and report any MAS CASs with the radio and purple smoke

JTAC

JTACs serve as the communicators between the squad and the air forces (referred to as Reaper forces). There is one JTAC per squad.

  1. Complete the JTAC training and monitor the JTAC Channel for changes that could affect your job
  2. Stay on the long-range air channel (32) and respond to all calls to your squad
  3. Communicate any needs of your squad to Reaper FAC, including but not limited to CAS strikes, Resupplies, Redeployments, Pick-ups

2IC

2IC stands for second in command. They assist the squad leader and step up as the squad leader if the squad lead becomes unconscious/dead.

  1. Same as Trooper
  2. Step up to squad leader should the squad lead go unconscious/die, including stepping up on the long-range ground radio (70) to communicate with other ground forces

Squad Lead

The role of a Squad Lead (SL) is to manage an entire squad. These individuals tend to micromanage LESS and focus more on general orders to complete current tasks which allow the 2IC to be creative in completing said tasks.

  1. Lead the squad based on incoming ground information and decisions made in the planning stage with the platoon staff
  2. Communicate updates over the long-range ground channel (70) to ensure your squad and others are up to date on what is happening
  3. Relay orders to the team leads, who will pass this information on to their teams to complete
  4. Keep an up to date roster of your squad inside your squad folder
  5. Update the promotion board each week with any member that is due for a promotion
  6. Ensuring all administrative documents (including, but not limited to this document) are kept up to date
